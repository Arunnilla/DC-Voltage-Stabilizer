NE555 and LM2596

Using NE555 Timer for PWM in a Buck Converter

The NE555 timer is a popular IC that can be used to generate PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) signals. In a buck converter, we need PWM to control the switching of the transistor (MOSFET) — and the duty cycle (percentage of ON time) controls the output voltage. So: NE555 = PWM Generator → controls Buck Converter switching

How it Works:

1. NE555 configured in Astable Mode:

he NE555 runs as a free-running oscillator, continuously producing a square wave (ON and OFF pulses).

2. PWM Generation:

By adjusting the timing resistors and capacitors, you change the duty cycle (how long ON vs OFF). Higher duty cycle = Higher output voltage in buck converter.

3. Switch Control:

The PWM output from the NE555 is connected to the gate of a MOSFET (or transistor). The MOSFET acts like a fast electronic switch for the buck converter circuit.

4. Buck Converter Operation:

When PWM signal is HIGH (ON), the MOSFET conducts, inductor stores energy. When PWM signal is LOW (OFF), the MOSFET turns off, and the inductor supplies the load.

Important Points:

Frequency: The NE555 must generate PWM at a frequency high enough (around 20kHz to 100kHz for practical buck converters). (Too low frequency will cause large ripple.)

Duty Cycle Control: You can add a potentiometer in the NE555 circuit to adjust the duty cycle manually — this way you can adjust output voltage!

Quick Formula for NE555 PWM Frequency:

When in astable mode: f = 1.44/((Ra + 2Rb) x C) Where: Ra and Rb are resistors, C is the timing capacitor.

What is LM2596?

LM2596 is a DC-DC buck converter IC that can step down a higher DC input voltage to a lower regulated DC output voltage. It is a switching regulator (not linear like LM317), so it is highly efficient (typically 80-90%). It's widely used in ready-made adjustable buck converter modules you find online!

onstruction (Internal Blocks of LM2596)

  • Inside the LM2596 IC, there are:
  • High-speed Switch (Internal transistor)
  • PWM Controller (to control duty cycle)
  • Oscillator (typically 150kHz switching frequency)
  • Feedback Circuit (to keep output voltage stable)
  • Protection Circuits (like thermal shutdown, overcurrent protection)

External Components needed:

  • Inductor
  • Diode
  • Capacitor
  • Resistors and Potentiometer

Working of LM2596

1. Input Voltage (Vin): You apply a DC voltage (up to 40V) to the input pin.

2. Switching Operation: LM2596 turns its internal switch ON and OFF at high speed (150kHz). It controls the duty cycle to adjust the amount of energy sent to the load.

3. Inductor and Diode: Inductor stores energy when the switch is ON. Diode provides current path when the switch is OFF.

4. Output Voltage (Vout): A stable and smooth DC voltage is produced at the output. You can adjust the output voltage (if using adjustable type) by varying a potentiometer or setting feedback resistors.

Key Features of LM2596:

  • Input Voltage: 4V to 40V
  • Adjustable Output Voltage: 1.23V to 37V (for adjustable version)
  • Output Current: Up to 3A
  • Switching Frequency: ~150kHz
  • High Efficiency: Up to 90%
  • Built-in Protection: Thermal shutdown, current limiting
